The 60 / 30 / 10 read

Most companies waste budget putting large language models where a simple script would work faster and more reliably.

A Series A narrative, a new youth partnership in the UK, and a quiet drift toward clinical adjacency all change the shape of the operational stack behind the platform. The Eduba view on i-BrainTech’s stack breaks down like this.

60%
Traditional code

CRM updates, onboarding playbooks, session-data exports, multilingual content. Postgres and a cron job do this better than any LLM.

30%
Rule-based logic

Neural-data governance policy, return-to-play decision boundaries, data-retention schedules. Rules, reviewed by humans, logged.

10%
Genuine AI layer

Turning each club engagement into a published outcome asset without Konstantin writing it. This is where the value is.

A recent comparator

Feeld is the closest shape. Product-company CTO, small technical team, operationalizing AI internally.

Delivery was a scoped sprint: one workshop, a handful of advisory calls, an Organizational Context Architecture, and a Strategic Operations Framework.

The output was not a new platform. The output was a map of where each kind of work belongs, written once, owned by the team, and usable for the next round of hires.

What lands in the first 30 days

  • Workshop with the founding team and sales leadership
  • Organizational Context Architecture written against the existing stack
  • Strategic Operations Framework for the next 12 months
  • Case-study production workflow sized for a non-founder owner
  • Neural-data governance scaffolding: retention, access, separation of duties

Governance reference point

The sports positioning can become a medical positioning in a single customer contract.

For i-BrainTech the adjacency is clinical. That raises the bar on evidence and governance. Eduba’s Ethics Engine (arxiv.org/abs/2510.11742, repo at github.com/RinDig/AuditEngine) is a psychometric assessment tool for evaluating ideological and moral patterns in LLMs, and it is the right reference point precisely because the sports positioning can become a medical positioning in a single customer contract.

Below the orchestration layer

Where the work turns out to be a data pipeline rebuild.

Eduba partners with NLP Logix for work that sits below the orchestration layer. NLP Logix has been in machine learning since 2011 and runs over 150 data scientists.

If the right answer at i-BrainTech turns out to be a production session-data pipeline (longitudinal EEG tracking across athletes, classifier retraining, or a data-warehouse migration as you scale from 14 countries to 30) rather than an architectural sprint, the partnership makes that route available without a new vendor search.
